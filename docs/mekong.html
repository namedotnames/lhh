The Mekong River

Excessive dams on the Mekong River, along with climate change, have upset one of Asia's largest ecosystems.

When China began building dams, it changed the flow of the river, leading to extreme flooding and droughts for the countries downstream.

An uptick in salinity in the river is forcing locals to dig deeper for drinking water.

In the process, pockets of chemicals which release arsenic into the river and drinking water are being hit, leading to high levels of poisoning and cancer.

In one extreme case, Van Phuc, a suburb of Hanoi, has 300 times the acceptable level of arsenic in the water.

What was supposed to be a boon for the economy has become a mortal danger, creating climate refugees and threatening communities that have lived off this river for thousands of years.
